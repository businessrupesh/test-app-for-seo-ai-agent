Over-ear, on-ear, or in-ear?

The first decision is form factor. Over-ear headphones cup your entire ear, on-ear sit on top of it, and in-ear sit inside your ear canal. Each has trade-offs in comfort, isolation, sound quality, and portability.

Wireless or wired?

Wireless headphones use Bluetooth to talk to your phone or laptop. Wired headphones use a cable. Wireless is more convenient. Wired is usually cheaper and has zero latency. Modern Bluetooth codecs are good enough that most listeners cannot tell the difference.

Noise cancellation

Active noise cancellation uses microphones to listen to ambient sound, then plays an inverted waveform to cancel it. It works best on constant low-frequency sounds like a plane engine or office HVAC. It cannot cancel a person yelling next to you.

Battery life

Battery life matters more than people expect. Look for at least 20 hours on wireless. Most reasonable headphones today claim 30-40 hours, which is enough that you only charge them once a week.

Comfort

You will be wearing these for hours. Clamping force, weight distribution, and earpad material matter as much as sound quality. If a pair sounds great but hurts after an hour, you will not use them.

Headphones live a hard life. They get crammed into bags, dropped, sat on, and tangled. Look for replaceable parts (especially earpads and cables), and check the warranty terms.
